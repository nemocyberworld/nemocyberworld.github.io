OSINT Investigation — Step-by-step

A sequenced checklist of tools & techniques to run investigations responsibly and with OPSEC.

Showing — items
01

Google Smartly — Advanced Search (Dorking)

Discovery Core: site:, filetype:, intitle:

Use advanced Google operators to uncover exposed files, directories, or scoped results.

  • Examples: site:facebook.com "Full Name", filetype:pdf site:gov.bd "budget", intitle:index.of
  • Goal: find public documents, open directories, or scoped content related to target.
02

Search by Email, Username, or Name

Identity Tools: WhatsMyName, HaveIBeenPwned, Hunter

Start with the identity pivot: email ↔ username ↔ name. Use these to expand to social profiles and breaches.

  • WhatsMyName / Maigret / Sherlock — scan username across platforms.
  • HaveIBeenPwned, Dehashed, Scylla — check breach exposure.
  • Hunter.io — find email patterns for a domain.
03

Analyze Images & Location (Image / Geo OSINT)

Media Tools: Google / Yandex / Exif.tools

Extract EXIF/metadata, reverse-image, and geolocate visual clues.

  • Google Reverse Image, Yandex Image Search for visual matches.
  • Exif.tools / ExifTool to extract GPS, timestamp, device info.
  • Use GeoSpy / SunCalc / Mapillary / Yandex Street View to verify and triangulate locations.
04

Social Media OSINT

Profiles Tools: Twint, Picuki, InstaLooker

Collect public social media traces, posts, friends, and metadata.

  • Use platform-specific tooling or scrapers (Twint for Twitter, Graph APIs where permitted).
  • Picuki / InstaLooker for anonymous Instagram viewing.
  • Map a target's network and note access patterns/timezones.
05

Core OSINT Resources & Learning

Learning OSINT Framework, Bellingcat, YouTube

Keep a shortlist of canonical resources and practice challenges.

  • OSINT Framework, Bellingcat OSINT Guide, SANS OSINT materials.
  • YouTube channels: I-Intelligence, Hacksploit Bangla (for Bengali content).
  • Practice: Trace Labs CTFs, Hacktoria, OSINT Dojo.
06

Metadata Extraction & Manipulation

Forensics Tools: ExifTool, strings, binwalk, FOCA

Extract timestamps, GPS, software markers, and hidden strings from files.

  • ExifTool for images & docs. Example: exiftool image.jpg
  • strings / binwalk for binaries and firmware.
  • FOCA for document metadata collected via Google Dorks.
07

Passive Infrastructure Recon

Infra Shodan, Censys, DNS mapping

Map domains, IPs, tech stacks and exposed devices without active probing.

  • Shodan / Censys for exposed devices and banners.
  • DNSDumpster, SecurityTrails, ViewDNS for passive DNS and history.
  • BuiltWith / Wappalyzer for tech stack fingerprinting; ASN lookups for related hosting ranges.
08

Sock Puppets & Operational Security (OPSEC)

OPSEC VMs, Tor, VPN, compartmentalization

Protect your identity when investigating. Separate personas and artifacts.

  • Use VMs (Tails, Qubes), chain VPN → Tor for anonymity when needed.
  • Create believable sock puppets with legitimate backstories if interacting.
  • Strip EXIF and cookies before uploading images; never search targets on personal devices.
09

Advanced Image & Video Geolocation

Geo SunCalc, Mapillary, topography

Use shadows, architecture, weather and terrain to verify location and time.

  • SunCalc.net to confirm sun angle vs timestamp.
  • Mapillary / Yandex / Street View to visually match scenes and road signs.
  • Topographic maps & flight path data for disaster/strike verification.
10

Social Network Mapping

Mapping Maltego, SpiderFoot, Recon-ng

Build relationship graphs of people, domains, and organizations.

  • Maltego for graph-based relationships; use SocialLinks, IntelX transforms as available.
  • SpiderFoot / Recon-ng for automated scraping and enrichment.
  • Use Sherlock / WhatsMyName to enumerate aliases across platforms.
11

Investigate Corporate & Financial Info

Corporate OpenCorporates, Aleph, LittleSis

Trace ownership, directors, subsidiaries and offshore structures.

  • OpenCorporates for registrations; Companies House for UK filings.
  • Aleph (OCCRP) for leaked docs, sanctions, and corruption cases.
  • LittleSis to map political and corporate connections.
12

Email, Breach & Credential OSINT

Breach HaveIBeenPwned, Dehashed, EmailRep

Check exposure, leaked dumps, and reputation of email addresses.

  • HaveIBeenPwned / Dehashed / Scylla / LeakCheck to locate leaks and dumps.
  • EmailRep.io and IntelX for reputation and historical presence.
  • Document findings, but do not attempt to access accounts or use leaked credentials.
13

Dark Web & Forum OSINT (Cautious)

Darkweb Tor, IntelX, Hunchly

Search darknet resources for leaks and actor activity — with strict OPSEC.

  • Use Tor Browser and dark-web indexes (Ahmia, DarkSearch.io) safely.
  • Monitor relevant forums (avoid downloading illegal content).
  • Use Hunchly for archiving and maintaining an evidence chain.
14

Blockchain OSINT

Blockchain Etherscan, Chainalysis, TRM

Trace transactions, cluster wallets, and identify laundering behavior.

  • Use Etherscan, Blockchain.com, BitQuery for public chain analysis.
  • For advanced attribution use Chainalysis / TRM Labs (paid).
  • Look for mixers, clustering patterns, and reuse of addresses across services.
15

Build Your Own OSINT Stack (Automation)

Automation Python, SpiderFoot, Recon-ng

Automate repetitive tasks and stitch tool outputs together.

  • Learn Python for API integrations and custom scrapers.
  • Use SpiderFoot and Recon-ng to orchestrate multi-source scans.
  • Export results to CSV/JSON for downstream analysis (Neo4j, Kibana).
16

Maltego — Transform-Based Graph Analysis

Graph Transforms, clustering, OPSEC

Use the right entity, add transforms carefully, and manage graph size.

  • Start from a relevant entity: person, email, domain, phone, company.
  • Use built-in transforms and add SocialLinks/IntelX/VirusTotal where needed.
  • Use entity coloring, collections, and explore mode for focus; export annotated graphs for reporting.
  • OPSEC: do not run transforms from personal identity/IP — use proxies and API keys safely.
17

SpiderFoot & Recon-ng

Automation CLI & GUI workflows

Automate broad reconnaissance and chain modules for deeper sweeps.

  • SpiderFoot for wide scans across 100+ data sources (use CLI for automation).
  • Recon-ng for modular, scriptable recon with API key support; export CSVs for analysis.
  • Run modules selectively to avoid noise (and rate limits).
18

SocialLinks, IntelX & Specialized Tools

Deep search social media, leaks, historic snapshots

Use SocialLinks for deep social media & dark-web content; IntelX for leak archival.

  • SocialLinks CE / Pro for aggregating forum/profile data, face search (Pro).
  • IntelX selectors (e.g., selectors:email_address name@example.com) to search leaks and paste clones.
  • Combine outputs with HaveIBeenPwned / Dehashed for comprehensive breach mapping.
19

EXIFTool + SunCalc + Mapillary Combo

Verification metadata → visual matching

Combine metadata extraction with solar geometry and street imagery for high-confidence geolocation.

  • Extract EXIF with exiftool image.jpg; load GPS into mapping tools.
  • Use SunCalc to verify lighting/shadow consistency with claimed timestamps.
  • Use Mapillary / Yandex to visually match structures and signage.
20

Shodan, Censys, FOFA & Breach Tools

Recon exposed devices, breach search

Use internet-wide search engines and breach repositories to complete the picture.

  • Shodan / Censys queries for exposed services; FOFA for obscure filters.
  • HaveIBeenPwned, Dehashed, Scylla / LeakCheck for breaches and dumps.
  • Keep logs and always archive with Hunchly / screenshots for evidence chain.
21

Hunchly — Archiving Your Investigation

Evidence screenshots, timestamps, URLs

Archive every step (screenshots, metadata) to retain an auditable chain of evidence.

  • Hunchly auto-captures pages, timestamps, and can highlight relevant keywords.
  • Useful for legal reporting and team handoff.
22

Obsidian, Graph Notebooks & Offline Graphs

Triage Obsidian, Neo4j, GraphXR

Store notes, build local graphs, and index findings for revisit and reporting.

  • Obsidian for markdown-based note-taking and Dataview indexing.
  • Use Neo4j or GraphXR if you need offline graph analytics similar to Maltego.
  • Keep exports of datasets, screenshots and annotated timelines in secure storage.
23

Final: OPSEC Golden Rules & Reporting

Policy legal & ethical boundaries

Before concluding: ensure ethical handling and prepare reports.

  • Never search real targets on personal devices; separate identity and tools.
  • Use burner emails/phones for operational profiles if interaction is necessary.
  • Archive everything, annotate evidence, and prepare clear reports (PDF/GraphML) for stakeholders.
  • Do not access or use leaked credentials — document but do not exploit.
